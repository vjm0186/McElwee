Filing Will


A Will is a document which disposes of a person's property after his/her death, it generally 
names a person's representative or administrator of the estate.

When to file a Will:

The custodian of the deceased's original Will must deposit the Will with the Circuit Court in 
the County where the decedent was domiciled within 10 days after learning of the person is 
deceased.

The custodian must supply the person's Death Certificate with a person's Social Security number
to the clerk upon deposit the original Will.

You do not need an attorney to file the original Will with the Clerk of the Circuit Court.  
However you may want to consult with an attorney before filing the original Will. The attorney 
should be able to tell whether or not Probate is necessary based upon the wording of the Will 
and the Trust.

Probate is necessary only when things are unclear as to how to make distributions.
