   <h3>Notifications</h3>
    <br>
    <pre>
A Will is a document which disposes of a person's property after his/her death it generally names a personal representative to administer the estate.

The custodian of the deceased original Will must deposit the Will with the Clerk of the Circuit Court in the 
county where the decedent was domiciled within 10 days after learning the person is deceased.

The custodian must supply the person's death certificate with a person's Social Security number to the clerk
upon deposit of the Well.

You do not need an attorney to file the Will with the Clerk of the Circuit Court.  However you may want to 
consult with an attorney before filing the Will.  The attorney should be able to tell whether or not Probate is 
necessary based upon the wording of the Will and the Trust.

Probate is necessary only when things are unclear as to how to make distributions.
    </pre>
